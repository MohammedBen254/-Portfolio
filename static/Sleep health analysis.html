Introduction¶

Sleep is a fundamental aspect of human well-being, playing a crucial role in physical and mental health. Understanding the factors that influence sleep patterns and overall lifestyle can provide valuable insights into promoting healthier living. The Sleep Health and Lifestyle dataset under consideration comprises a comprehensive collection of information related to individuals' sleep habits, daily activities, and various lifestyle factors. This dataset offers a multifaceted view of contributors to sleep quality, duration, and associated health indicators.

Objective:

The primary objective of analyzing this dataset is to uncover patterns, correlations, and potential factors influencing sleep health and lifestyle. By examining relationships between variables such as sleep duration, quality of sleep, physical activity and stress levels.

Signification of each column :¶

Here's the role of each column in the provided dataset:

  1. Person ID: A unique identifier for each person in the dataset.
  2. Gender: The gender of the individual, typically categorized as male or female.
  3. Age: The age of the person, usually in years.
  4. Occupation: The person's job or profession, such as Software Engineer, Doctor, or Sales Representative.
  5. Sleep Duration: The average number of hours the person sleeps per night.
  6. Quality of Sleep: A subjective measure of the quality of the person's sleep, often rated on a scale.
  7. Physical Activity Level: The person's level of physical activity, which may be quantified on a scale or described categorically (e.g., low, moderate, high).
  8. Stress Level: A measure of the person's stress or perceived stress levels, often rated on a scale.
  9. BMI Category: The Body Mass Index (BMI) category of the individual, which typically classifies them as Normal, Overweight, Obese, etc.
  10. Heart Rate: The person's heart rate, often measured in beats per minute (BPM).
  11. Daily Steps: The number of steps the person takes in a day, indicating their physical activity.
  12. Sleep Disorder: Indicates whether the person has a sleep disorder, such as Sleep Apnea, or if they have None (no sleesleep patterns.
